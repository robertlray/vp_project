FIRST VIDEO PORTRAIT 1971
My work in video portraiture began in 1971 while working in Sucromu, a West African bush community. One morning as I was setting up my video portapac, a young boy from the village stood in front of my camera. He stared silently into the lens. It was at this moment I realized that the face is in a constant state of flux; the real time video exposes and makes public the sequences of silent thoughts.

CIVIL RIGHTS PIONEERS
Civil Rights Pioneers Rosa Parks and Cesar Chavez portraits were both made in 1989. Graciously, both Mrs. Parks and Mr. Chavez agreed to sit for their video portraits in silence. The viewer is now able to observer for him or herself, these two exceptional Americans who fought for our human dignity. Later I took my portrait of Rosa Parks from 1989 and joined it with the 2009 ceremonial Inauguration of President Obama 2009.

5 COMPOSERS 5 COUNTRIES:
England, Italy, France, Spain and USA. 1988

Original Length: 3-5 minutes each, 8mm video. 1988. Five Composers, Five countries Original Length: 305 minutes, 8mm video. 1988.
Five Composers, Five Countries, portrays five contemporary composers from five separate countries. Each composer was commissioned to write a three-minute mini opera to be collectively produced by their own nation. My OTV (Opera TV) was to be seen like MTV. The operas were never made, but their remains small video portraits of each composer as well the documentation of the storyboard creation for each of the five operas.
